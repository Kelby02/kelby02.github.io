Tenebrism


Developed by Michelangelo Merisi da Caravaggio in 16th century Italy, tenebrism is an extreme form of chiaroscuro based around the use of pure darkness to embolden works with dramatic impact. The black darkness is so strong and overbearing it almost becomes the focal point of the image. This uber dramatic spotlight effect is what defines tenebrism, as chiaroscuro is a more general term that also encompasses the use of light and shadow to create a volume and three-dimensionality in art.




I see tenebrism in the works of Jean-Michel Basquiat,in each of these paintings the heavy black is used to create different visuals, however each time it is for the purpose of bolstering the drama or emotional intensity.
